What to expect before you step in.

Can I keep my current CRM or do I have to switch?

You keep your CRM. We work with HubSpot, Pipedrive, Monday, Airtable, and most stacks founders already run. The problem is usually not the tool — it's the structure inside it. We rebuild the structure, not the software.

My team is already overwhelmed — will this add more work before removing any?

Short answer: a little, yes. For the first two weeks your team spends 2–3 hours/week in handoff sessions. After that, they're running systems that save each person 4–8 hours/week. Net positive by week three — but we won't pretend week one is free.

I've tried automations before and they broke more than they fixed. What's different?

Most automation projects fail because they automate a broken process. We fix the process first, then automate what's worth automating. If a workflow shouldn't exist at all, we kill it — not automate it.

How long until I actually stop being the bottleneck?

Most founders feel the shift by week four — team stops pinging them for basic decisions. Full backend handoff takes 60–90 days depending on how deep the founder dependency runs. It's not a switch; it's a move.
